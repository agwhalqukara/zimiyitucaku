Gabriel Basso Is Portrayed As Peter Sutherland In The Night Agent

The Night Agent is a highly awaited new action drama series that premiered solely on Netflix on Thursday, March 23. The series, directed by Shawn Ryan, is based on author Matthew Quirk’s well-known book of the same name.

Netflix’s formal short summary for the series’ first season is as follows:

“A vigilant FBI agent answers a call while monitoring an emergency line, which leads him into a deadly conspiracy involving a White House mole.”

Season 1 of The Night Agent, which consists of ten episodes, has received a lot of favorable attention from the audience due to its engaging and thoughtful plot and outstanding acting performance by the show’s main performer Gabriel Basso, who plays Peter Sutherland in the series. Since Netflix released the official teaser for the drama series, viewers have been eager to learn more about performer Gabriel Basso and his varied previous works.

Learn everything there is to know about performer Gabriel Basso and his work as The Night Agent season 1 premiere on Netflix.

Gabriel Basso, born Louis Gabriel Basso III, is a 28-year-old American performer who began his career as a child actor in the 2007 film Meet Bill, where he portrayed the unnamed part of a child with cancer. He rose to fame, however, as Adam Jamison in the 2010 comedic drama series The Big C. Basso is also known for his roles as Martin Read in the 2011 film Super 8, Patrick Keenan in the 2013 film The Kings of Summer, Gooch in the 2015 film Barely Lethal, Jimmy Petersen in the 2016 film American Wrestler: The Wizard, and J. D. Vance in the 2020 film Hillbilly Elegy.

In addition to Alice Upside Down, The Whole Truth, Alabama Moon, The Hive, Ithaca, and Anatomy of the Tide, the performer has appeared in a number of other well-known films. He has also played key roles in several notable TV shows, including The Middle, iCarly, R. L. Stine’s The Haunting Hour: The Series, The Red Road, Perception, and a few others.

Basso’s character, Peter Sutherland, works for the FBI in The Night Agent. Along with him, actor Luciane Buchanan portrays Rose Larkin, as a crucial character. Shawn Ryan, the show’s senior director, stated during an interview about the two leads:

“Peter is more of an old-school rule follower, while Rose is more of a new-school rule breaker…They need to learn to be more like each other.”

In addition to Shawn Ryan, the film’s senior producers include Marney Hochman, Julia Gunn, Seth Gordon, William Sherak, James Vanderbilt, and a few others.
